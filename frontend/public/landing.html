Claims 1–3 are rejected under 35 U.S.C. §103 as being unpatentable over Anderson (US 9,876,543)1 in view of Chen (US 2019/0123456)2.

Anderson discloses “a processor configured to handle interrupt requests using a priority queue” (col. 4, lines 23–45)3, and, the Examiner asserts, teaches dynamic reordering of that queue (col. 7, line 2)4.

    Three citations confirmed against the record. One characterization the passage does not support, flagged before it ever reached your draft. That is the whole product.
