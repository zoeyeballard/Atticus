Claim Rejections - 35 USC § 103

Claims 1-3 are rejected under 35 U.S.C. § 103 as being unpatentable over
Anderson (US 9,876,543 B2) in view of Chen (US2019/0123456 A1).

Regarding claim 1, Anderson discloses a processor configured to handle interrupt
requests using a priority queue (col. 4, lines 23-45). Anderson does not explicitly
disclose dynamically reordering the priority queue based on task deadlines. However,
Chen teaches dynamic deadline-based reordering of pending tasks (paragraph [0032],
Fig. 3). It would have been obvious to one of ordinary skill in the art at the time of
the invention to modify Anderson to incorporate the deadline-based reordering of Chen
in order to improve real-time responsiveness.

Claims 2-3 depend from claim 1 and are rejected for the same reasons.
